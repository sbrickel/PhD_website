Research interests

My professional interest lie in the description of reactive dynamics in gas and condensed phase.

For my work I apply molecular dynamics simulations, including surface crossing algorithm for bond breaking and formation (reactive molecular dynamics). I am interested in the effects of solvation on the potential energy surfaces and therefore the influence of solvation on many processes during dynamics of (bio)chemical reactions.

In addition I am working on competitive reaction pathways, that include multi-surface approaches. After starting with calculations on small molecules in solution my research expands to enzymatic reactions, using advanced sampling methods.

Furthermore I am involved in computation biochemical studies, including docking, ancestral sequence reconstruction, and (hidden) Markov models.
